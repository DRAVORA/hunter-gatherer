Important Safety Information

This application is intended for informational and educational purposes only. It is NOT a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always seek the advice of your physician or other qualified health provider before beginning any exercise programme.

1. Not Medical Advice

ATAVIA and its content, including training programmes, readiness assessments, exercise instructions, and recommendations, are provided for general informational purposes only. The App does not provide medical advice and is not intended to be used for medical diagnosis or treatment.

The information in this App:

  • Is not a substitute for professional medical advice
  • Should not be used to diagnose or treat any health condition
  • Does not create a doctor-patient or therapist-client relationship
  • May not be appropriate for your individual circumstances

2. Consult Your Physician

You must consult with a qualified healthcare professional before starting any exercise programme, particularly if you have any pre-existing health conditions.

You should consult a physician before using ATAVIA if you:

  • Have any cardiovascular conditions (heart disease, high blood pressure, etc.)
  • Have any musculoskeletal conditions or injuries
  • Have diabetes or metabolic disorders
  • Are pregnant or postpartum
  • Have been sedentary for an extended period
  • Are over 40 years of age and have not exercised regularly
  • Have any chronic health conditions
  • Are taking any medications that may affect your ability to exercise
  • Have any concerns about your physical ability to exercise safely

3. Assumption of Risk

Physical exercise carries inherent risks. By using ATAVIA, you acknowledge and accept that:

  • Exercise involves risk of injury, including but not limited to muscle strains, sprains, fractures, joint injuries, and cardiovascular events
  • You are voluntarily participating in physical activities at your own risk
  • You are solely responsible for your health and safety during exercise
  • Results from exercise vary and are not guaranteed
  • You should use your own judgement regarding the suitability of any exercise

Stop Exercising Immediately If You Experience:

  • Chest pain or pressure
  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Dizziness, lightheadedness, or feeling faint
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Sharp or sudden pain in any joint or muscle
  • Heart palpitations or irregular heartbeat
  • Extreme fatigue or exhaustion
  • Blurred vision or seeing spots
  • Loss of coordination or balance
  • Any other unusual symptoms

If you experience any of these symptoms, stop exercising immediately and seek medical attention.

4. Exercise Safely

To reduce your risk of injury when using ATAVIA:

  • Always warm up before and cool down after exercise
  • Use proper form and technique for all exercises
  • Start with lighter weights and progress gradually
  • Follow the stop rules provided in the App
  • Listen to your body and rest when needed
  • Stay hydrated before, during, and after exercise
  • Exercise in a safe environment with appropriate equipment
  • Do not exercise when ill, injured, or excessively fatigued
  • Seek instruction from a qualified fitness professional if unsure about any exercise

5. Readiness Assessments

The daily readiness assessments in ATAVIA are designed to provide general guidance based on self-reported data. These assessments:

  • Are not medical evaluations
  • Do not diagnose any health conditions
  • Are based solely on information you provide
  • Should be used as one factor in your decision to exercise, not the only factor
  • Do not replace your own judgement about your readiness to train

Even if the App indicates you are "ready" to train, you should not exercise if you feel unwell, are injured, or have any concerns about your ability to exercise safely.

6. Training Programmes

The training programmes in ATAVIA are general fitness programmes designed for healthy adults. These programmes:

You are responsible for selecting exercises and weights appropriate for your fitness level and any physical limitations you may have.
